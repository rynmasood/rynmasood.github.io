Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 10, 2026

Mappr is an Android mobile application developed and operated by an individual developer. This Privacy Policy explains what information I may process when you use the app, why I process it, how it may be shared with service providers, and what choices you have.

1. Summary

Mappr processes location-related information because location is core to the app's functionality.

Depending on how you use the app, Mappr may process:

  • Location data from your device to show your position in the app and support location-based features.
  • Search terms and map/routing requests that you submit.
  • Saved places, favorites, and route history that you choose to store locally on your device.
  • Crash, diagnostics, analytics, advertising, consent, and subscription-related information through third-party SDKs and service providers.

Mappr does not require you to create an account, and I do not ask you to submit your name, postal address, or phone number to use the app.

2. Information We Collect

A. Location Data

Mappr requests the following Android permissions where relevant to app functionality:

  • ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION
  • ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE_LOCATION

When granted, Mappr may access your device location to:

  • Center the map on your current position.
  • Show your location on the map.
  • Improve search relevance.
  • Reverse geocode coordinates into readable addresses.
  • Create routes and simulate movement along those routes.
  • Save your last-used map position and related settings locally on your device.

Mappr also supports mock-location functionality. If you use that feature, the target coordinates you select and related route points may be processed locally and, when needed, sent to third-party routing or geocoding providers to calculate routes or return addresses.

B. User-Provided Content

The app may process information you actively enter or select, including:

  • Location search queries.
  • Selected coordinates.
  • Saved place names and addresses.
  • Favorites.
  • Route start points, end points, waypoints, and route history.
  • Preferences such as map type, theme, distance unit, location accuracy, randomization settings, and schedule settings.

C. Locally Stored App Data

Mappr stores certain data locally on your device using on-device storage technologies such as Room and Android DataStore. Based on the current implementation, local storage may include:

  • Last selected latitude and longitude.
  • Camera position, zoom, bearing, and tilt.
  • Mocking state and schedule settings.
  • Map type, distance unit, theme, accuracy, and randomization preferences.
  • Saved locations, favorites, and location history.
  • Route history, including route addresses, waypoint count, route geometry, total distance, estimated duration, and speed.
  • Credit balance and premium/subscription cache state.
  • The last-seen version of in-app announcements.

D. Diagnostics, Analytics, and App Performance Data

Mappr includes Firebase services and Google mobile SDKs that may automatically collect certain technical and diagnostic data, depending on your device configuration, app version, and applicable SDK behavior. This may include:

  • App instance information.
  • Device model and operating system information.
  • Crash logs, stack traces, and performance diagnostics.
  • Basic usage and event data.
  • IP address and network-related metadata used by service providers to deliver their services.
  • Device or app identifiers used by Google or Firebase SDKs for analytics, diagnostics, fraud prevention, or advertising operations.

Current dependencies and integrations relevant to this category include Firebase Analytics, Firebase Crashlytics, and Firebase Remote Config.

E. Advertising, Consent, and Subscription Data

Mappr integrates Google AdMob, Google User Messaging Platform (UMP), and Google Play Billing.

These services may process data such as:

  • Consent choices regarding ads in supported jurisdictions.
  • Advertising-related identifiers or signals made available by Google SDKs.
  • Ad request and ad delivery metadata.
  • Subscription product details, purchase status, purchase tokens, and entitlement status handled through Google Play.

Mappr gates ad preloading behind the UMP consent flow where applicable. Subscription purchases are processed by Google Play, not directly by Mappr.

3. How I Use Information

I use information to:

  • Provide core map, search, geocoding, routing, and mock-location features.
  • Save your preferences and restore your last app state.
  • Maintain favorites, history, and route records you choose to keep on your device.
  • Support subscription features and restore premium status.
  • Show ads and manage ad consent where permitted by law.
  • Configure app messaging and notices through Remote Config.
  • Monitor crashes, diagnose bugs, improve stability, and maintain security.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce applicable terms and policies.

4. Location Data and Mapping-Specific Processing

Because Mappr is a mapping and navigation-style app, location processing is central to the service.

How location is processed

  • Your device location may be read locally from Android location services when you grant permission.
  • Coordinates you choose on the map may be sent for reverse geocoding to turn them into a readable address.
  • Text search requests may be sent to search providers to return matching places.
  • Route calculations may send route coordinates to third-party routing infrastructure.
  • The app may locally persist your last location, selected destination, saved places, and route history so the experience can be restored later.

Local storage of location information

Based on the current app implementation, the following may be stored locally on your device until deleted or reset:

  • Last viewed or selected location.
  • Favorite places and search/history entries.
  • Route history and route geometry.
  • Mock-location preferences and related scheduling state.

Third-party routing and geocoding

To provide search, reverse geocoding, or route calculation, Mappr may send queries and coordinates to third-party providers, including:

  • OpenStreetMap Nominatim for location search and reverse geocoding.
  • OSRM routing infrastructure for route generation.
  • Google geocoding functionality exposed through Android platform geocoder services.
  • Other third-party map providers we may enable over time, such as Mapbox.

If Mappr transitions search or other map functions to another provider such as Mapbox, that provider may process the same types of data needed to answer your request, such as search text, selected coordinates, approximate or precise location, device/network metadata, and request logs.
